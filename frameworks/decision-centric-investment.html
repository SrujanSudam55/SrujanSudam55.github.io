Decision-Centric Investment Framework

Most investment conversations drift into tools, use cases, and capability shopping. This framework forces a different order: decision first, ownership second, then support, then durability.

Decision criticality

Decision supportability

4
Decision durability

Stress-test for scale, incentives, regulation, and edge cases.

1) Decision criticality

If you cannot name the decision, you are not investing yet. You are browsing.

  • Which decision are we improving, in plain language?
  • What changes if it improves (cost, risk, growth, customer outcome)?
  • How often does this decision recur, and at what scale?

Example: FS credit policy exceptions. TMT content escalation decisions.

2) Decision ownership

Ownership is not who sponsored the pilot. Ownership is who is accountable when outcomes disappoint.

  • Who owns the decision on a bad day?
  • What is the challenge line for borderline cases?
  • Who has authority to override, and who audits overrides?

This is the step most AI programs skip, then pay for later.

3) Decision supportability

Not every decision benefits from automation. Choose the smallest support that changes the outcome.

  • Information for visibility (dashboards, alerts).
  • Recommendation for prioritisation (ranking, scoring).
  • Automation only when reversibility and risk are controlled.

If the decision is political or ambiguous, start with recommendation, not automation.

4) Decision durability

A win that collapses at scale is not a win. Durability is the strategy.

  • What happens under volume spikes or stress events?
  • What incentives will people optimise around?
  • What regulatory or audit expectations apply?

Durable systems treat edge cases as first-order design inputs.

Common failure modes

  • Tool-first investment You buy capability before you own the decision, then retrofit value later.
  • Use case sprawl Teams run pilots in parallel, but leadership cannot steer because no decision spine exists.
  • Feasibility mistaken for value A model works, so it ships. Outcomes do not improve because the decision was never defined.
  • Ownership dissolves after the pilot When edge cases appear, responsibility becomes conditional and escalations return.
