A leadership framework to greenlight, gate, or kill Data and AI investments.

The Investment Spine

Most AI programs die the same way. They produce activity without commitment. The org becomes “busy” evaluating tools, exploring use cases, and running pilots. But no one has named the outcome, owned the downside, or set the gates that make scale possible.

Executive intent
If the downside is not owned, the investment is not real. This framework makes ownership, controls, and durability explicit before scale.

1) Define the Spine

The spine is the single outcome leadership will defend. Not “better insights”. Not “AI maturity”. One outcome, one unit of measure, one line of accountability.

  • What outcome will change?Profit, loss, risk exposure, churn, throughput, fraud loss, capital efficiency.
  • How will it be measured?Metric, baseline, target, and time window.
  • What gets stopped if this wins?A real investment replaces something. If nothing stops, it is additive theatre.

2) The Gates

Gates prevent “pilot success, scale failure”. A gate is not a meeting. It is a condition that must be true.

Gate 1
Ownership

Who owns the decision when the system is wrong, not just when it performs.

  • Single accountable ownerNamed. Empowered. Visible.
  • Challenge lineClear rule for what must be escalated vs passed.
  • Override rightsWho can override, who audits overrides, and why.
Gate 2
Controls

Especially critical in FS: auditability, explainability, and evidence. In TMT: trust, safety, and policy consistency.

  • Audit trailCan you reconstruct why a decision happened?
  • Exception handlingBorderline cases do not become politics.
  • Risk postureWhat failure is acceptable, and what is not.
Gate 3
Durability

Scale, stress, incentives, drift. This is where most “wins” collapse.

  • Stress conditionsVolume spikes, outages, adversarial behavior.
  • Incentive driftWhat will people game once it is measured?
  • Operating ownershipWho maintains, monitors, and improves it.

3) Scorecard (2 minutes)

This is the part executives actually need: a fast read on whether the investment is safe to scale. Score it honestly. The output is a recommendation.

This is intentionally strict. It is designed to stop pilot theatre.

Use this in Financial Services and TMT

Financial services example

Credit exceptions, fraud actions, collections triage, risk approvals. The investment is only real when override rights, audit trails, and failure thresholds are owned.

  • If you cannot defend a bad outcome in an audit, you are not ready to scale.
  • If overrides are common, the decision boundary is unclear.

TMT example

Content escalation, pricing decisions, retention offers, ad quality controls. Scale breaks when incentives drift and edge cases become policy fights.

  • If policy interpretation differs across teams, automation multiplies inconsistency.
  • If metrics can be gamed, models will optimise the wrong behavior faster.
