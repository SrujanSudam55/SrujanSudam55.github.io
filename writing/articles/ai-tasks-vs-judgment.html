AI and advanced analytics are rarely the bottleneck. Decision clarity is.

AI: Tasks vs Judgment

Most companies think the problem is AI or advanced analytics. It isn’t.

The real issue is simpler and more expensive: they do not know which decisions they want to improve, or what impact they actually need.

Dashboards are not the work

Dashboards and reports are comforting because they look like progress. They create motion. They create meetings. They create metrics.

But without clarity, analytics becomes noise. Not because the data is wrong. Because the organisation never agreed what the data is for.

Most organisations already have dashboards, reports, and plenty of data. What they often lack is a decision spine.

What is usually missing

In practice, the gap shows up in three places.

  • Outcomes that data must drive Not “better visibility”. Real outcomes. What changes if this decision is improved, and how will you know it did?
  • A roadmap beyond description A deliberate move from descriptive analytics (“what happened?”) to advanced analytics (“what will happen and what should we do?”), with ownership at every stage.
  • A scaling strategy A plan to scale analytics as a capability, not a set of isolated experiments that die when the sponsor changes.

When analytics becomes transformative

Advanced analytics becomes transformative when it is aligned with strategy.

That alignment is not a slogan. It is a sequence: name the decision, define “good”, assign ownership, then build the smallest support that actually changes the outcome.

That is when analytics stops being a reporting layer and starts acting like leverage. Leaders anticipate challenges earlier. Trade-offs become visible sooner. Options become comparable. Growth opportunities appear because choices become clearer, not because charts become prettier.

The value of analytics is not the insight. It is the decision that becomes unavoidable.

The uncomfortable truth about AI

After all the buzz around AI, one truth stands out.

Without strong advanced analytics foundations, AI cannot thrive, at least not in a way that creates sustainable business value.

AI on top of unclear decisions does not create strategy. It creates faster confusion, with better formatting.

What I find most interesting

The most exciting part of this work is helping leadership teams connect data to decisions.

Making analytics a driver of transformation, not a mirror that reflects the past with higher resolution. The shift is subtle, but it is everything: from “what do we know?” to “what are we deciding, and who will own it when it gets messy?”

Closing

If you want to explore this in your context, start with one decision: high stakes, recurring, and currently handled through escalation or intuition.

Once you name it and assign ownership, the analytics roadmap becomes obvious. Until then, the organisation will keep buying tools to avoid making a choice.
